Comparison of token counts across parts (b), (d) and (e):
Tokenizing the whole book gives 333590 tokens made up of 24226 unique word types (part b). Removing English stopwords barely changes the number of unique types (24084) because stopwords are a small closed set of very frequent words, but it removes many token occurrences (172803 tokens left), which is why the top of the frequency ranking changes the most. Stemming with the Porter stemmer keeps the total number of tokens the same as in (b) (333590) but collapses inflected forms such as "travel", "travels" and "travelling" onto a single stem, cutting the vocabulary sharply to 18303 unique stems. As a result the stemmed frequencies are higher per type and the log-log Zipf curve stays straight but shifts left, since fewer distinct types now carry the same total mass.
